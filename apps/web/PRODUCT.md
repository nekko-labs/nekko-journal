# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who have tried daily journaling and habit-streak apps (Day One, Stoic, Habitica, Streaks) and dropped off because the daily cadence became a chore. They still want to reflect, track goals, and keep a record — but on a rhythm they can actually sustain. They sit down with Nekko Journal roughly **once a month**, often in a calm moment (evening, weekend), to look back on the month, capture highlights and the occasional struggle, add a few photos, nudge their yearly goals, and skim how the year is trending. The job to be done: *remember and shape my life at a month scale, without the guilt machinery of daily apps.*

## Product Purpose

A monthly journaling and goal-tracking app. Set goals for the year, break them into months, capture each month's highlights/struggles/photos/trackers, and look back across months and years. Monthly cadence is the core feature, not a limitation — long enough that showing up isn't a burden, short enough to remember what happened. Success = the user returns month after month, year over year, and can look back on a rich, true record they're glad to have.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, warm, unhurried.** Voice is a gentle, encouraging friend — never a coach barking streaks, never a clinical tracker. Three words: *calm, personal, honest.* Emotional goals: arriving feels like opening a paper journal on a quiet evening (warmth, safety, a little pride); leaving feels lighter, not behind. Optional things stay optional and visually quiet — the app never pressures.

## Anti-references

- **Streak/habit guilt machinery** (Habitica, Streaks, Duolingo-style "don't break the chain"): no streak counters that shame, no red broken-streak states, no daily push nagging.
- **Clinical/data-dense quantified-self dashboards**: not a spreadsheet of metrics; numbers serve reflection, not the other way around.
- **Bloated productivity SaaS** (heavy Notion-style chrome): no cluttered toolbars or settings mazes.
- **Generic AI-cream SaaS landing aesthetic**: avoid the look of "every AI-generated app in 2026."

## Design Principles

- **Monthly ritual is sacred.** Every decision respects that the user shows up ~12 times a year. No daily nags, no streak guilt; reward returning, never punish absence.
- **Optional stays quiet.** Struggles, mood, gratitude are first-class-but-de-emphasized; empty is always a fine answer.
- **Your data, your device.** Local-first and fully functional offline; cloud is a convenience, never a hostage.
- **Calm over clever.** Familiar, trustworthy product patterns; delight reserved for moments (the year-in-review, a month coming to life), not sprinkled everywhere.
- **Look back is the payoff.** The record you build is the product; surfacing it (year grid, timeline, year-in-review) is a first-class job, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1 on its (warm, tinted) background — guard against muted-gray-on-cream failures. Mood is never conveyed by color alone (emoji + label accompany the color). Full keyboard navigation; visible focus rings. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` (entrances and micro-interactions degrade to instant/crossfade). Light and dark themes, both warm. Touch targets ≥40px on mobile.
